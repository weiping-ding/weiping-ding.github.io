WeiPing Ding

Weiping Ding (M'16-SM'19) received the Ph.D. degree in Computer Science, Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (NUAA), Nanjing, China, in 2013. He was a Visiting Scholar at University of Lethbridge(UL), Alberta, Canada, in 2011. He was a Post-Doctoral Fellow / Research Fellow / Visiting Scholar at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Alberta (UA), Alberta, Canada in 2014, at the Brain Research Center, National Chiao Tung University (NCTU), Hsinchu, Taiwan from 2014 to 2015, in Hong Kong Polytechnic University (HKPU), Hong Kong in 2015, at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineer, National University of Singapore (NUS), Singapore in 2016, and at Computational Intelligence and Brain Computer Interface (CIBCI) Center, CAI, University of Technology Sydney (UTS), Australia from 2017 to 2018. Now, Dr. Ding is the Chair of IEEE CIS Task Force on Granular Data Mining for Big Data. He is a member of Senior IEEE, IEEE-CIS, ACM, CCAI and Senior CCF. He is a member of Technical Committee on Soft Computing of IEEE SMCS, on Evolving Intelligent Systems of IEEE SMCS, on Granular Computing of IEEE SMCS, and on Data Mining and Big Data Analytics of IEEE CIS.

Dr. Ding is currently a Full Professor with the School of Information Science and Technology, Nantong University, Nantong, China?and also the supervisor of Master and Ph.D postgraduate by the Faculty of Data Science at City University of Macau. His research interests include deep neural networks, granular data mining, uncertainty modeling in intelligent transportation systems, rough sets theory, fuzzy sets theory, big data optimization and analysis for Various real application scenarios, and medical images analysis.

Dr. Ding has published more than 150 research peer-reviewed journal and conference papers in this field, including IEEE Transactions on Fuzzy Systems, IEEE Transactions on Neural Network and Learning System, IEEE Transactions on Evolutionary Computation, IEEE Transactions on Cybernetics, IEEE Transactions on Industrial Informatics, IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems, IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computational Intelligence, IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering, IEEE Transactions on Cognitive and Developmental Systems, etc, and he He has co-authored two books and held 20 approved invention patents. His nine authored or co-authored papers have been selected as ESI Highly Cited Papers (i.e. listed in Top 1% globally in the corresponding discipline).

Dr. Ding is vigorously involved in editorial activities. He serves/served on the Editorial Advisory Board of Knowledge-Based Systems (Elsevier) and Editorial Board of Information Fusion (Elsevier), Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence (Elsevier), and Applied Soft Computing (Elsevier). He serves/served as an Associate Editor of IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, IEEE Transactions on Fuzzy Systems, Information Sciences (Elsevier), IEEE/CAA Journal of Automatica Sinica, Neurocomputing (Elsevier), Swarm and Evolutionary Computation (Elsevier), IEEE Access and Journal of Intelligent & Fuzzy Systems, and Co-Editor-in-Chief of Journal of Artificial Intelligence and System. He is the Leading Guest Editor of Special Issues in several prestigious journals, including IEEE Transactions on Evolutionary Computation, IEEE Transactions on Fuzzy Systems, IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computational Intelligence, Information Fusion, Information Sciences, Applied Soft Computing and so on. He has delivered more than 30 keynote speeches at international conferences and has served as the Program Chair, Workshop Chair, or Program Committee Member of several international conferences and symposiums in the area of data mining, fuzzy decision-making, and knowledge engineering.
